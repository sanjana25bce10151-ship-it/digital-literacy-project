Part A: Case Study – The "Urgent KYC" UPI Scam

Topic: UPI Fraud & Phishing

Word Count: Approx. 225 words

Scenario: In January 2026, a college student received a text message stating that their bank account would be blocked within 2 hours unless they updated their KYC (Know Your Customer) details. The message contained a link that looked official: bit.ly/bank-kyc-update-2026.

What Happened: Anxious about their account being frozen, the student clicked the link, which led to a fake website mimicking a popular Indian bank's login page. The student entered their mobile number, bank customer ID, and—most critically—their UPI PIN. Within minutes, three unauthorized transactions were made, draining ₹15,000 from the account. The scammers used a "Request Money" feature on a UPI app, which the student mistakenly authorized, thinking it was a verification step.

Consequences: The student lost their semester savings and experienced significant mental stress. Because the student had voluntarily entered their PIN on a third-party site, the bank initially struggled to reverse the transaction.

Conclusion & Prevention: This case highlights the danger of "Social Engineering." To prevent this, students must remember that banks never ask for a UPI PIN to receive money or update KYC. Always verify links through official bank apps and never act in haste when receiving "urgent" alerts.
